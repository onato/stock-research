## Company Overview

Sprouts Farmers Market is a specialty grocer focused on natural, organic, and "better-for-you" products. Key characteristics:

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Store Count | 464+ stores across 24 states |
| Headquarters | Phoenix, Arizona |
| Employees | ~35,000 |
| Store Format | ~20,000-25,000 sq ft (smaller than traditional grocers) |
| Fresh Mix | 60% (vs 40% at traditional grocers) |
| Target Customer | Health-conscious, affluent consumers |
| Positioning | 10-15% below Whole Foods pricing |

### Dividend Payout Ratio

**SFM does not pay dividends.** Capital is returned via share buybacks:

- $1B buyback authorization (August 2025)
- ~$450M annual buyback capacity
- Represents ~6% of float annually at current prices

**For DCF modeling:**
- Use 0% for explicit dividends
- Or model 25-35% "total shareholder return" to capture buyback economics
- Terminal assumption: 50% as growth normalizes

**If SFM delivers even half their recent growth rate, significant upside exists from both:**
1. EPS growth
2. Multiple re-rating

## Conclusion

### Investment Case Summary

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Fundamentals | Strong (37% ROE, net cash, $500M FCF) |
| Growth | Intact but decelerating from 38% |
| Valuation | De-risked at 14.9x P/E |
| Risk/Reward | Favorable at current entry |

### Bottom Line

**15% CAGR is achievable** from the $77 entry point, requiring:
1. Same-store sales normalizing to 4-5% (not the 1% Q4 guide)
2. Store growth executing at 10% annually
3. No further multiple compression

**The key variable is Q1 2026 earnings.** If comps stabilize at 3-5%, the stock likely re-rates. If consumer weakness persists, further downside possible but limited given already depressed valuation.

**At current prices, you don't need multiple expansion to achieve 15% returns — just EPS execution.**
