## Part 2: What is AML/CFT and Why Does It Matter?

### AML = Anti-Money Laundering
### CFT = Combating the Financing of Terrorism

**What these laws require:**

Financial firms MUST have systems to:

### 1. Know Your Customer (KYC)
- Who are you doing business with?
- Are they legitimate?
- Are they who they say they are?
- Verify identity documents
- Understand source of funds
- Document everything

### 2. Monitor Transactions
- Are transactions normal for this customer?
- Are there suspicious patterns?
- Are funds coming from legitimate sources?
- Track unusual behavior
- Flag anomalies
- Investigate red flags

### 3. Report Suspicious Activity
- If something looks wrong, report it to authorities
- Don't just ignore red flags
- Have a system to catch unusual activity
- File SARs (Suspicious Activity Reports)
- Cooperate with law enforcement
- Don't tip off the customer

### 4. Keep Records
- Document everything
- Prove you did your checks
- Be able to show regulators your work
- Retain for 5-7 years
- Audit trail required
- Regular reviews

---

### Why This Matters So Much

**AML/CFT violations are taken VERY seriously** because:

- Criminals use financial systems to "wash" illegal money
- Terrorists need to move money to fund operations
- Drug cartels, human traffickers, corrupt officials all need banking
- Regulators have ZERO tolerance for this
- Post-9/11, post-2008 financial crisis, enforcement is aggressive
- Reputational damage is severe

**Recent examples of AML fines:**

| Institution | Year | Fine | Country |
|------------|------|------|---------|
| ING Bank | 2018 | ‚Ç¨775M | Netherlands |
| ABN AMRO | 2021 | ‚Ç¨480M | Netherlands |
| Danske Bank | 2022 | Billions | Denmark |
| HSBC | 2012 | $1.9B | USA |
| Deutsche Bank | 2017 | $630M | USA/UK |
| Standard Chartered | 2019 | $1.1B | USA/UK |
| Westpac | 2020 | $1.3B AUD | Australia |

**These are MASSIVE penalties:**
- Career-ending for executives
- Company-threatening events
- Sometimes criminal prosecutions
- License revocations possible
- Business restrictions common

**The Dutch regulators (DNB/AFM) are particularly aggressive:**
- ING: ‚Ç¨775M (2018) - largest fine in Dutch history at the time
- ABN AMRO: ‚Ç¨480M (2021) - plus ongoing criminal investigation
- Both major banks, both survived, but huge reputational damage
