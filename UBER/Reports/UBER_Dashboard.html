Uber Technologies (UBER)

Financial Dashboard | Updated January 23, 2026

Company Overview

Uber operates the world's largest ride-sharing and food delivery platform, connecting consumers with drivers and restaurants across 70+ countries. The company operates through three main segments: Mobility (ride-hailing, 57% of revenue), Delivery (Uber Eats, 33%), and Freight (logistics, 10%). Uber has achieved sustained profitability with Q3 2025 net income of $6.6B and continues accelerating growth with 189M monthly active users and 3.5B quarterly trips.

Business Model

Asset-light platform business model connecting supply (drivers, restaurants) with demand (riders, eaters) through a mobile app, earning revenue primarily through service fees and commissions. The company has achieved scale with 189 million monthly active users and 3.5 billion trips per quarter as of Q3 2025.

  • Mobility (Ride-sharing) 57%
  • Delivery (Uber Eats) 31%
  • Freight 12%

Competitive Position

Market Position: Dominant in U.S. ride-sharing (76% market share), #2 in U.S. food delivery (23-26% share behind DoorDash's 67%), global leader in combined mobility and delivery

Competitors: Lyft (ride-sharing 24-30% U.S.), DoorDash (67% U.S. food delivery), Regional players (DiDi, Grab, Ola), Waymo/autonomous vehicles

Moat Factors:

  • Network effects: 189M monthly users + millions of drivers create liquidity advantage
  • Brand recognition: Synonymous with ride-sharing globally across 70+ countries
  • Uber One subscription: 36M members (up 60% YoY) spending 3.4x more than non-members
  • Data & algorithms: Years of ride data enable superior routing and dynamic pricing

Growth Drivers

  • Uber One subscription: 36M members generating $1B+ annual run rate, members spend 3.4x more
  • International expansion: Emerging markets in Latin America and Asia Pacific
  • Autonomous vehicles: Expanded Waymo partnership in Austin and Atlanta (launched 2025)
  • Delivery expansion: Beyond food into grocery, alcohol, and retail
  • Advertising platform: High-margin ad business leveraging consumer data
  • New mobility modalities: Bikes, scooters, public transit integration, EV transition

Key Risks

  • HIGH: Autonomous vehicle disruption - Waymo has 14% SF market share with 33% retention
  • HIGH: Gig worker reclassification could dramatically increase costs
  • MODERATE-HIGH: DoorDash dominance in delivery (67% vs 23-26% share)
  • MODERATE-HIGH: Consumer spending pressures - 52% reduced usage due to 7.2% price increases
  • MODERATE: Driver supply-demand imbalance requires continuous incentives
  • MODERATE: Currency exposure (46.3% revenue from international, 5.5pp Q1 2025 headwind)

Bull Case

  • Network effects strengthening: 189M users + 3.5B quarterly trips create powerful moat
  • Proven profitability: Second year of GAAP profitability with expanding margins (4.2% adj. EBITDA)
  • Multiple growth vectors: Mobility dominance, delivery growth, Uber One ($1B+ run rate), advertising, AV integration
  • AV opportunity: Waymo partnership positions Uber as platform layer for autonomous future
  • Secular tailwinds: Ride-hailing growing 16.1% CAGR to $287.6B by 2034, food delivery to $563.4B by 2029
  • Strong unit economics: Take rate expanding to 21.78% while maintaining trip growth

Bear Case

  • AV disruption risk: Waymo 14% SF share shows autonomous vehicles can compete. Uber's platform role not guaranteed
  • Consumer pressure mounting: 72% would stop using if prices rise further, Q3 2024 bookings missed expectations
  • Regulatory existential threat: Gig worker reclassification to employees could destroy unit economics
  • Weak delivery position: DoorDash 67% vs Uber Eats 23-26% share. DoorDash-Lyft partnership targets Uber One
  • Valuation requires execution: Premium based on 17-21% bookings growth. Currency headwinds create risk
  • Driver economics fragile: Take rates up to 21.78% (some studies claim 35-40%) pressure driver retention

Recent Developments

  • Q3 2025 (Nov 4): Record quarter - $49.7B gross bookings (+21% YoY), net income $6.6B (nearly tripled), 3.5B trips (+22% YoY)
  • Q2 2025 (Aug 6): Revenue $12.65B (+18% YoY), announced $20B share repurchase, Uber One reached 36M members (+60% YoY)
  • Q1 2025 (May 7): Revenue $11.5B (+14% YoY), net income swung to $1.8B profit, MAPCs grew 14% to 170M
  • Jan 2025: Waymo autonomous vehicles launched in Atlanta exclusively on Uber platform
  • Q4 2024: Uber One surpassed 30M members (up 70% YoY), generating 45% of delivery bookings
  • Feb 2024: Announced inaugural $7B share repurchase authorization

Macro & Industry Outlook

Industry: Global ride-hailing market at $74.9B in 2025, projected 16.1% CAGR to $287.6B by 2034. U.S. food delivery at $429.9B in 2025, growing to $563.4B by 2029.

Cyclicality: Moderately cyclical - ride-sharing and delivery are discretionary services that decline in recessions but have shown resilience.

Secular trends: Urbanization, shift from car ownership to Transportation-as-a-Service, autonomous vehicle adoption, restaurant digital transformation, EV transition (Uber target: 100% zero-emission by 2040).
