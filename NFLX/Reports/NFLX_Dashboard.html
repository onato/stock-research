Netflix (NFLX) Financial Dashboard

Streaming Entertainment Leader | Data as of January 2026

Company Overview

Netflix is the world's leading subscription streaming entertainment service with approximately 325 million paid memberships across over 190 countries. The company offers TV series, films, and games across a wide variety of genres and languages. Netflix generates revenue primarily through monthly membership fees, including both ad-supported and ad-free subscription plans, and is increasingly monetizing through advertising and live content such as WWE and NFL games.

Business Model

Subscription-based streaming model with monthly recurring revenue. Primary revenue from subscription fees (~90%+), with growing advertising revenue from ad-supported tier launched in 2022. Fixed-cost content model with multi-year licensing and production commitments.

Competitive Position

Main Competitors: Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, Max, Apple TV+, Paramount+, Peacock, Hulu, YouTube, Linear TV

Market Position: Market leader in paid streaming with 325M memberships. Holds ~19-20% US streaming market share, neck-and-neck with Amazon Prime Video (20%). Combined Disney properties represent 25% share.

Moat Factors:

  • Scale and global reach (325M members, approaching 1B viewers)
  • Content library and original programming ($18-20B annual investment)
  • Technology and personalization (proprietary algorithms, own CDN)
  • Brand recognition and customer loyalty (first-mover advantage)
  • International localization capabilities (content in 50+ countries)

Growth Drivers

  • Advertising revenue doubling from $1.5B to ~$3B (190M ad tier users)
  • Password sharing enforcement added ~50M subscribers since May 2023
  • Live content: WWE Raw ($500M/year), NFL Christmas games
  • International expansion (50%+ of content budget on international)
  • Pricing power demonstrated with Jan 2025 increases across all tiers
  • Content franchises and IP expansion (consumer products, games)
  • Margin expansion targeting 31.5% operating margin for 2026
  • Strong FCF generation (~$11B expected in 2026) enables buybacks

Key Risks

  • High: Content performance and quality risk with 90% original content
  • High: Competitive intensity from Disney, Amazon, Apple with deep pockets
  • High: Mature market saturation in UCAN and EMEA limiting growth
  • High: Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition antitrust review uncertainty
  • Moderate: Password sharing enforcement backlash and subscriber churn
  • Moderate: Advertising model execution risk and premium tier cannibalization
  • Moderate: Content cost inflation (10% YoY increase to $20B)
  • Moderate: High debt load ($15.7B) and FX exposure (60%+ revenue outside US)
  • Moderate: European content quotas, levies, and data privacy regulations

Bull Case

  • Advertising inflection: Ad tier scaling to 190M users, revenue doubling to $3B, potential $5-10B+ within 3-5 years
  • Pricing power and margin expansion: Jan 2025 price increases successful, 31.5% operating margin target with path to 35%+
  • Market leadership and scale: 325M members approaching 1B viewers creates sustainable competitive advantage
  • Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition upside: $72-83B deal adds massive content library and franchises if approved
  • Strong cash generation: $11B FCF enables aggressive buybacks, potential dividend initiation
  • International growth runway: Low penetration in LATAM and APAC, $8B+ local content investment
  • Live content differentiation: WWE and NFL create appointment viewing, reduce churn, command premium ad rates
  • Subscription stickiness: Recurring revenue with strong retention, viewed as essential entertainment

Bear Case

  • Market saturation: High penetration in core markets, Q2 2024 added only 4.8M subs (down from 15M in Q4 2023)
  • Competition eroding share: Prime Video (20%) ahead of Netflix (19%), Disney portfolio at 25% combined
  • WBD acquisition risks: $72-83B deal faces antitrust review, integration of declining legacy businesses
  • Content cost inflation: 10% YoY increase to $20B outpacing 12-14% revenue growth guidance
  • Ad tier cannibalization: 55% of new signups choosing $7.99 ad tier vs. $17.99 standard ($10/month ARPU loss)
  • Gaming strategy failure: After 4+ years, gaming <0.5% engagement, AAA studio shutdown in 2024
  • Regulatory headwinds: European quotas, levies, data privacy fines, operating in 190+ countries
  • Economic sensitivity: Premium pricing ($24.99 top tier) may hit consumer resistance in recession
  • Valuation risk: Trading at premium multiples, any miss could trigger multiple compression

Recent Developments

  • 2026-01-20: Q4 2025 earnings: Record 19M subscriber additions to 325M total. Guides FY2026 revenue $50.7-51.7B (12-14% growth), 31.5% operating margin, ~$11B FCF
  • 2025-12: Announces $72-83B acquisition of Warner Bros. Discovery, triggering 12-18 month antitrust review
  • 2025-12-25: Second annual NFL Christmas Day games stream with 30M+ viewers
  • 2025-11: Ad-supported tier reaches 190M monthly users (doubled from 94M in May 2024), 55% of new signups
  • 2025-01-21: Price increases across all US tiers: ad-supported $6.99→$7.99, standard $15.49→$17.99, premium $22.99→$24.99
  • 2025-01-06: WWE Monday Night Raw debuts exclusively on Netflix under $500M/year, 10-year deal
